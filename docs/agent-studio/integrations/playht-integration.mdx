
# PlayHT Integration

`PlayHT` is a key player in artificial intelligence (AI) voice generation technology. They're a renowned producer of quality text-to-speech (TTS) and voice cloning services.

Understanding the great potential of `PlayHT`, we at `SmythOS` have developed bespoke components to streamline your integration journey with `PlayHT`. This guide offers a comprehensive walk-through on leveraging these components effectively.

## PlayHT 2.0 - TTS

Shining a spotlight on a pivotal feature within `SmythOS`, let's delve into the prowess of the `PlayHT 2.0 - TTS` component. Crafted with innovation in mind, this component stands as a streamlined solution for effortlessly transforming text into speech.

### Settings

#### `Secret Key` and `User ID`

To initiate the use of this feature, you'll first need to configure your `Secret Key` and `User ID`. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Create a `PlayHT` account if you don't already have one.
2. Navigate to your account control panel and generate your `Secret Key` and `User ID`. You can do this by clicking [here](play.ht/studio/api-access).
3. Input this `Secret Key` and `User ID` in the specified section in the feature's settings.

> **Note:** Treat the `Secret Key` and `User ID` as sensitive data. In `SmythOS`, you can securely manage it using the `Vault` feature. For more details, please refer to [this](https://app.smythos.com/doc/#/vault/keysManagement) section in the documentation.

#### Output Format

The service supports a variety of output formats for the speech file:

- `mp3`: A commonly used format for consumer audio streaming or storage.
- `wav`: An audio file format that retains the full quality of the original recording.
- `ogg`: A free, open container format providing efficient streaming and manipulation of high-quality digital multimedia.
- `flac`: An audio format similar to MP3, but lossless, meaning that audio is compressed without any loss in quality.
- `mulaw`: An audio encoding format used in telephony systems and commonly used for voice encoding.

#### Quality 

The quality of the generated speech can be specified by choosing from the following options:

- `draft`: Suitable for initial drafts or non-critical applications.
- `low`: Basic audio quality for non-demanding applications.
- `medium`: Standard audio quality that balances file size and fidelity.
- `high`: Enhanced audio quality for clearer and more professional results.
- `premium`: Highest audio quality with the most natural and life-like sound.

#### Emotion 

Personalize the speech by selecting an emotion tone:

- **Female Voices:**
  - `female_happy`: A cheerful and jubilant tone.
  - `female_sad`: A sorrowful and dismal tone.
  - `female_angry`: A heated and exasperated tone.
  - `female_fearful`: A scared and apprehensive tone.
  - `female_disgust`: A repulsed and disdainful tone.
  - `female_surprised`: An astonished and amazed tone.

- **Male Voices:**
  - `male_happy`: A joyful and elated tone.
  - `male_sad`: A downcast and melancholic tone.
  - `male_angry`: A fierce and fervent tone.
  - `male_fearful`: A panic-struck and fearful tone.
  - `male_disgust`: A scornful and aversive tone.
  - `male_surprised`: A shocked and astounded tone.

#### Speed

Control how fast the generated audio should be. A number greater than 0 and less than or equal to 5.0

#### Sample Rate

A number greater than or equal to 8000, and must be less than or equal to 48000

#### Voice Guidance

A number between 1 and 6. Use lower numbers to reduce how unique your chosen voice will be compared to other voices. 

#### Style Guidance

A number between 1 and 30. Use lower numbers to to reduce how strong your chosen emotion will be. Higher numbers will create a very emotional performance.

### Inputs

This feature requires two default inputs:

1. **text `required<string>`:** This is the textual data to be converted into speech.
2. **voice_id `required<string>`:** The identifier for the desired voice to be used in the audio generation. This parameter comes with a predefined default value.

<Image src="/img/playht/screenshot2.png" alt="alt text" />

> **Note:** With the flexibility and customization in mind, `SmythOS` allows you to expand the default input list by adding new inputs using the `+` button situated in the `Inputs` section.

### Outputs

Upon using this component, you'll receive two default outputs:

1. **Response:** This is the received data, in this case, a speech representation of the input text. 
2. **Headers:** These are the HTTP Headers included in the `PlayHT` server's response.
3. **id:** The unique identifier assigned to the process that was executed.

<Image src="/img/playht/screenshot3.png" alt="alt text" />

> **Note:** Similar to inputs, you can add new outputs by clicking on the `+` button in the `Outputs` section. This feature can come in handy when you aim to extract specific values from the `Response`. Please refer [here](https://app.smythos.com/doc/#/components/APICall?id=example-without-authentication) to see a practical example.
